The MacConnochie–McConihe descent — index

Companion to the evidence chart (mcconihe-family-tree.html). Part I indexes every person in the research, alphabetically, with evidence tier and sources. Part II indexes the surname variants. Part III gives the full source list. Tier markers: documented · documented person, line untracked · tradition or conjecture · unknown.

Part I — Persons

Campbell, Alexander, 1st of Kilmartin · d. 1729
Eldest son of Lt-Col Dougall by Janet MacNeill; received Kilmartin 1674/75. His line continued to modern times, latterly using "MacDhonnachaidh" as a middle name. [RBS3 p.791 · DC]
Campbell, Archibald · son of the Tutor, fl. 1613
Named with his brother Dugald as "sons to the tutor of Inverawe" in the Privy Council complaint of 30 Nov 1613 (Colquhoun of Luss v. Clan Gregor and assisters). Untracked thereafter. A leading candidate ancestor of the Ulster/McConihe line. Note: Diarmid Campbell's volume saw an "Archibald son of the Tutor" reference and read it as the laird; Gordon MacGregor's fuller reading of the 1613 entry restores him. [RPC · GM corr.]
Campbell, Archibald of Inverawe · fl. c. 1570
Laird of Inverawe; father of Dougall (laird), John dubh (the Tutor), Patrick, and Duncan. [GM corr. · DC]
Campbell, Archibald of Inverawe, "the pupil" · d. bef. April 1648
Infant heir of Dougall (d. c. 1583); his minority made his uncle John dubh Tutor. Credited in family tradition with killing the Tutor on coming of age. Father of seven sons including Lt-Col Dougall, John (of Achaworran), and Patrick. [DC · GM corr.]
Campbell, Archibald of Inverawe · d.s.p. 21 May 1705
Last laird of the direct line; Governor of Duart for Argyll; summoned 1682 over the forfeited Argyll estate; family took refuge on Fraoch Eilean after the 1685 rising. Married (1) Mary MacNeill 1657, (2) Lillias Campbell of Lawers 1701. Died without legitimate heirs; Inverawe passed via his brother Duncan of Crunachy's son and was sold 1765. [DC · RBS3 p.781]
Campbell, Beatrice (of Stronchormaig) · m. 1656
Eldest daughter of the late Dugald Campbell of Stronchormaig (a MacConnochie cadet line); married Rev. John Duncanson of Kilmartin by contract of Oct–Nov 1656, Dugald Campbell of Inverawe a party. [W pp.52–53 · F]
Campbell, Dugald · son of the Tutor, fl. 1613
The second "rebel" brother in the 30 Nov 1613 Privy Council entry; untracked thereafter. Candidate ancestor of the Ulster/McConihe line. [RPC · GM corr.]
Campbell, Dougall of Inverawe · laird, d. c. 1583
Elder brother of the Tutor; his death leaving the infant "pupil" created the tutorship. [DC]
Campbell, Lt-Col Dougall of Inverawe · d. 1666
Officer, then second-in-command (Lieutenant-Colonel), of the Marquess of Argyll's regiment raised 1642 for service in Ireland — the true kernel behind the 1867 memoir's "second in command of the Earl of Argyle's army." Father of Archibald (d. 1705), Duncan of Crunachy, John, Alexander of Kilmartin, and Dugald of Shirvan. [DC · RBS3 p.781 · IM]
Campbell, Dugald, 1st of Shirvan · d. Dec 1730
Son of Lt-Col Dougall by Janet MacNeill; baillie of Nether Lorne for Breadalbane; first of the Shirvan cadet line. [RBS3 p.798]
Campbell, Duncan, progenitor · late 1300s
Son of Gillespic (Archibald) Campbell of Lochawe; eponym of the kindred — descendants are Mac Dhonnchaidh, "sons of Duncan." Diarmid Campbell's "Duncan Skeodnasach" candidate and MacGregor's Duncan "from whom the Stronchormaig branch are said to descend" are the same man. A Great Seal charter of 15 Mar 1369 names an ancestral Duncan in the barony of Lochawe — possibly the kernel of the memoir's "1369" date. [DC · RBS3 (Campbell of Argyll) · IM]
Campbell, Duncan of Crunachy · d. bef. 27 Jan 1703
Brother of Archibald (d. 1705); of Crunachy in the Pass of Brander; baillie of Tiree for the Countess of Argyll; received conveyance of Inverawe lands 1686 when Archibald lacked male issue; his son Archibald succeeded to Inverawe. [RBS3 p.781 · DC]
Campbell, John · "brother to the Laird," fl. 1694, d. bef. 1710
Brother of Archibald (d. 1705); bond of caution as an officer of the court, 16 Oct 1694. In Scotland throughout the emigration window — not the immigrant. [RBS3 p.781]
Campbell, John, "Iain og" · son of the Tutor
Only legitimate son of the Tutor, by Barbara Graham; named with his mother in her 1607 bond to George Schaw as she left for the Lowlands; married the widow of William M'Clellan of Mullock. No descendants traced. Root of the conjecture that the Maconochies of Meadowbank descend from him (DC pp. 84–85). [DC · GM corr.]
Campbell, John, of Torgormaig & Ardtornish · b. c. 1644
Only son of Patrick (killed of Inverlochy wounds, 1645) and Mary Cameron, the "Fair Maid of Callart." Tenant in Mull, later tacksman of Ardtornish, Morvern (1684); m. Florence Cameron of Callart, who died Jan 1686 leaving two daughters. Examined and set aside as a candidate father of the immigrant. [DC · RBS3]
Campbell, John dubh MacConnochie, Tutor of Inverawe · c. 1557 – bef. July 1607
Second son of Archibald (fl. 1570); guardian of Inverawe c. 1583–1604 and de facto head of the kindred. In 1596 English intelligence reported him ready to lead 1,000 men to Ireland to aid Tyrone and O'Donnell — he had "friends in Ireland." Said to have been killed by the pupil on his coming of age. Father of Iain og, Archibald, and Dugald. [DC · GM corr.]
Campbell, Lillias (of Lawers), Lady McConochie · m. 1701
Daughter of Sir James Campbell of Lawers; god-daughter of the Earl of Breadalbane; second wife (then widow) of Archibald of Inverawe. Her letters to Breadalbane survive in NRS GD112 (e.g. GD112/39/203/17). [DC ch.11 · NRS GD112]
Campbell, Patrick · d. of Inverlochy wounds, 1645
Younger son of the pupil; married Mary Cameron, the "Fair Maid of Callart"; father of John of Torgormaig. [DC]
Duncanson, Rev. John, minister at Kilmartin · c. 1630–1687
Admitted 1655; married Beatrice Campbell of Stronchormaig 1656; deprived 1662 (Restoration ejections); restored under the Indulgence 1670; recruited for Argyll's 1685 Rising and fled to Holland, dying 1687. Wimberley queried "? a M'Conachie"; secondary accounts say Stirlingshire origin (unverified). Surname = direct anglicization of Mac Dhonnchaidh. [F · W · DC]
Duncanson, Major Robert · of the Argyll Regiment
Son of Rev. John Duncanson and Beatrice Campbell of Stronchormaig; transmitted the orders for the Glencoe Massacre, 1692. [F]
Graham, Barbara · the Tutor's widow
Second wife of the Tutor; mother of Iain og; her 1607 bond to George Schaw, naming her son, marks their departure from Argyll for the Lowlands. [DC · GM corr.]
Maconochie, Allan, Lord Meadowbank · 1748–1816
Court of Session judge from 1796; his family acquired the Meadowbank estate, Midlothian, in the mid-1700s. The later Lord Meadowbank (Alexander) is the "chief of the clan" named in the 1867 McConihe memoir. Under the working hypothesis, cousins — not ancestors — of the American line. [IM · NRS NG3/4/31 · GM corr.]
McConihe, father of John · unknown, b. ~1630s–40s
The open research question. Leading candidate pool: descendants of Archibald or Dugald, the Tutor's rebel sons of 1613; secondary pool: untracked younger sons of the laird line (six sons of the pupil, d. bef. 1648). All documented main-line males of the window are accounted for in Scotland. [analysis of DC · RBS3 · GM corr.]
McConihe, John, the immigrant · b. 22 Dec 1669, Argyllshire
Family tradition: born Argyllshire 1669; household to Londonderry, Ireland, 1678; present in the 1689 siege; emigrated to Nutfield/Londonderry, N.H., 1718. Documented from arrival: home lot recorded 25 Feb 1722/3 as "M°Conoghy." [IM · BC · DB · ER II:80]
McConihe, Isaac · 1787–1867, Troy, N.Y.
Grandson of the immigrant (son of John of Merrimack); judge; his 1867 memorial volume preserves the family tradition — Inverawe, "Maconochy," the Argyll army command, and Meadowbank — in compressed and partly garbled form, each error traceable to a real event. [IM]

Part II — The name and its variants

Mac DhonnchaidhGaelic, "son of Duncan." The kindred name of the Inverawe Campbells, from the 14th-century progenitor Duncan. Borne alongside the legal surname Campbell.
MacConnochie · M'Connoquhie · McConochie · MacDonachieArgyll record spellings, 1500s–1600s. Often "alias Campbell" in charters ("Patrick MacDonochie alias Campbell," 1617). "McConochie" alone denotes the laird of Inverawe in the Earl of Argyll's correspondence. Caution: unrelated families carried similar live patronymics (e.g. Campbell of Oib; Loch Tay bonds).
DuncansonDirect English translation of Mac Dhonnchaidh. Borne by the Inveraray merchant family (conjectured MacConnochie stock, per DC) and by Rev. John Duncanson of Kilmartin.
MaconochieLowland scribal form; the Midlothian family of Meadowbank from the early 1700s.
McConaghy · MacConaughey · McConoghyUlster forms from the 1600s onward — "there the name is often spelt 'MacConaughey'" (DC p. 85). The form the immigrant carried to New England.
McConiheNew Hampshire scribal freezing of the Ulster pronunciation; first recorded "M°Conoghy" (1722/3), settling as McConihe in the Merrimack generation.
