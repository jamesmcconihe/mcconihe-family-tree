The MacConnochie–McConihe descent — index

Companion to the evidence chart (mcconihe-family-tree.html). Part I indexes every person in the research, alphabetically, with evidence tier and sources. Part II indexes the surname variants. Part III gives the full source list. Tier markers: documented documented person, line untracked tradition or conjecture unknown. The research goal throughout: identify the father of John McConihe, b. 22 December 1669.

Revised 13 July 2026
The 30 November 1613 Privy Council entry has been consulted directly in the printed register (RPC, 1st ser., X, pp. 177–179, via HathiTrust page images; NRS verification pending). Entries updated: Archibald and Dugald (sons of the Tutor, explicitly filiated); Patrick (outlawed with his nephews); the Tutor (1595 spuilyie, RD1/167 death terminus); Barbara Graham (bond citation); Duncan the Tutor's brother (1602 denunciation, new entry). New persons added: Lachlan and Duncan McDonachie Campbell, possible additional sons. New sources: [NRS-RD], [CCSNA].

Part I — Persons

Campbell, Alexander, 1st of Kilmartin · d. 1729 documented
Eldest son of Lt-Col Dougall by Janet MacNeill; received Kilmartin 1674/75. His line continued to modern times, latterly using "MacDhonnachaidh" as a middle name. [RBS3 p.791 · DC]
Campbell, Archibald · son of the Tutor, fl. 1613 documented · untracked
Explicitly styled "Archibald McDonachie Campbell, son of the Tutor of Innerraw" in the Privy Council complaint of 30 Nov 1613 (Colquhoun of Luss v. Clan Gregor and assisters) — now read in the printed register [RPC X:179]. Unrelaxed from hornings of 27 & 29 May and 1 & 3 June 1613 for "schamefull and cruell murthouris, slauchteris, thiftis, reiffis and oppressionis"; the Earl of Argyll, personally present, disclaimed him on oath and was assoilzied. Untracked thereafter. A leading candidate grandfather of the Ulster/McConihe line. Historiographical note: Diarmid Campbell's volume read an "Archibald son of the Tutor" reference as the laird; Gordon MacGregor's reading of the 1613 entry restored him, and the printed page confirms it. [RPC X:177–179 · GM corr. · RBS3 p.782 n.4774]
Campbell, Archibald of Inverawe · fl. c. 1570 documented
Laird of Inverawe; father of Dougall (laird), John dubh (the Tutor), Patrick, and Duncan. [GM corr. · DC]
Campbell, Archibald of Inverawe, "the pupil" · d. bef. April 1648 documented
Infant heir of Dougall (d. c. 1583); his minority made his uncle John dubh Tutor. Credited in family tradition with killing the Tutor on coming of age. In 1621 fined-list context he stood cautioner (£1000) for Archibald MacLachlan Olchallum VicDoniche of Aniha, charged with resetting the Clan Gregor [RPC XII:580 via RBS3] — the kindred's Gregor entanglement continued past 1613. Father of seven sons including Lt-Col Dougall, John (of Achaworran), and Patrick. [DC · GM corr. · RBS3]
Campbell, Archibald of Inverawe · d.s.p. 21 May 1705 documented
Last laird of the direct line; Governor of Duart for Argyll; summoned 1682 over the forfeited Argyll estate; family took refuge on Fraoch Eilean after the 1685 rising. Married (1) Mary MacNeill 1657, (2) Lillias Campbell of Lawers 1701. Died without legitimate heirs; Inverawe passed via his brother Duncan of Crunachy's son and was sold 1765. [DC · RBS3 p.781]
Campbell, Beatrice (of Stronchormaig) · m. 1656 documented
Eldest daughter of the late Dugald Campbell of Stronchormaig (a MacConnochie cadet line); married Rev. John Duncanson of Kilmartin by contract of Oct–Nov 1656, Dugald Campbell of Inverawe a party. [W pp.52–53 · F]
Campbell, Dugald · son of the Tutor, fl. 1613 documented · untracked
"Dougall McCondochie Campbell, brother of the said Archibald" — the second explicitly filiated rebel son in the 30 Nov 1613 entry, now read in the printed register [RPC X:179]. Same hornings, same disclaimer by Argyll, untracked thereafter. Candidate grandfather of the Ulster/McConihe line. [RPC X:177–179 · GM corr. · RBS3]
Campbell, Dougall of Inverawe · laird, d. c. 1583 documented
Elder brother of the Tutor; his death leaving the infant "pupil" created the tutorship. [DC]
Campbell, Lt-Col Dougall of Inverawe · d. 1666 documented
Officer, then second-in-command (Lieutenant-Colonel), of the Marquess of Argyll's regiment raised 1642 for service in Ireland — the true kernel behind the 1867 memoir's "second in command of the Earl of Argyle's army." Father of Archibald (d. 1705), Duncan of Crunachy, John, Alexander of Kilmartin, and Dugald of Shirvan. [DC · RBS3 p.781 · IM]
Campbell, Dugald, 1st of Shirvan · d. Dec 1730 documented
Son of Lt-Col Dougall by Janet MacNeill; baillie of Nether Lorne for Breadalbane; first of the Shirvan cadet line. [RBS3 p.798]
Campbell, Duncan, progenitor · late 1300s documented
Son of Gillespic (Archibald) Campbell of Lochawe; eponym of the kindred — descendants are Mac Dhonnchaidh, "sons of Duncan." Diarmid Campbell's "Duncan Skeodnasach" candidate and MacGregor's Duncan "from whom the Stronchormaig branch are said to descend" are the same man. A Great Seal charter of 15 Mar 1369 names an ancestral Duncan in the barony of Lochawe — possibly the kernel of the memoir's "1369" date. [DC · RBS3 (Campbell of Argyll) · IM]
Campbell, Duncan · the Tutor's brother, fl. 1598–1602 documentedNEW
Youngest of the four brothers (with Dougall, John dubh, and Patrick). Denounced rebel by the Privy Council, 6 August 1602, for the theft of five cows with calves from Andrew MacBaith in Hilton of Maler in November 1598 [RPC VI:442, via RBS3]. Untracked thereafter. [RBS3 · RPC VI:442]
Campbell, Duncan of Crunachy · d. bef. 27 Jan 1703 documented
Brother of Archibald (d. 1705); of Crunachy in the Pass of Brander; baillie of Tiree for the Countess of Argyll; received conveyance of Inverawe lands 1686 when Archibald lacked male issue; his son Archibald succeeded to Inverawe. (Minor textual wrinkle in RBS3: a Patrick listed under Duncan's children is styled "lawful son to John Campbell, brother of Archibald Campbell of Inverawe" — an internal inconsistency, possibly a typo or an ambiguous 1703 deed.) [RBS3 p.781 · DC]
Campbell, Duncan McDonachie · fl. 1613, brother of Lachlan documented · filiation unprovenNEW
"Duncane, his brother [i.e. Lachlan's], servitor to the said Archibald [son of the Tutor]" among the unrelaxed rebels of 30 Nov 1613 [RPC X:179]. The record does not filiate him to the Tutor; his service to Archibald and his position in the list make him either a further son or close household kin. Untracked after 1613. Within the candidate pool for the emigrant ancestor's father. [RPC X:179]
Campbell, John · "brother to the Laird," fl. 1694, d. bef. 1710 documented
Brother of Archibald (d. 1705); bond of caution as an officer of the court, 16 Oct 1694. In Scotland throughout the emigration window — not the immigrant. [RBS3 p.781]
Campbell, John, "Iain og" · son of the Tutor documented · untracked
Only legitimate son of the Tutor, by Barbara Graham; named with his mother in her bond of 15 July 1607 to George Schaw of Knockhill for 200 merks Scots [RD1/167] as she left for the Lowlands; married the widow of William M'Clellan of Mullock. No descendants traced. Root of the conjecture that the Maconochies of Meadowbank descend from him (DC pp. 84–85). [DC · GM corr. · RBS3 · NRS-RD]
Campbell, John, of Torgormaig & Ardtornish · b. c. 1644 documented · set aside
Only son of Patrick (killed of Inverlochy wounds, 1645) and Mary Cameron, the "Fair Maid of Callart." Tenant in Mull, later tacksman of Ardtornish, Morvern (1684); m. Florence Cameron of Callart, who died Jan 1686 leaving two daughters. Examined and set aside as a candidate father of the immigrant. [DC · RBS3]
Campbell, John dubh MacConnochie, Tutor of Inverawe · c. 1557 – bef. 15 July 1607 documented
Second son of Archibald (fl. 1570); guardian of Inverawe c. 1583–1604 and de facto head of the kindred — never owner, hence no sasine trail. Subject of a Privy Council complaint, 2 Dec 1595, by Charles Campbell (servant to Moray of Abercairney), for having "spuilyied the whole goods and plenishing" at Ardowaneck in Breadalbane and occupying it by force [RPC V:241, via RBS3]. In 1596 English intelligence reported him ready to lead 1,000 men to Ireland to aid Tyrone and O'Donnell — he had "friends in Ireland"; the Clan Campbell Society (North America) states he led the Scots gallowglasses fighting in Ireland at the end of the 16th century [CCSNA — basis being sought from Diarmid Campbell]. Said to have been killed by the pupil on his coming of age (c. 1603, tradition); the record shows only that he had died by 15 July 1607, the date of his widow's bond [RD1/167]. Father of Iain og (by Barbara Graham), Archibald, and Dugald (by an earlier marriage, suggested Agnes Graham); Lachlan and Duncan (1613) possibly two more. He visited Glenorchy at Finlarig in June 1597 with three sons. [DC · GM corr. · RBS3 · RPC V:241 · CCSNA]
Campbell, Lachlan McDonachie · fl. 1613 documented · filiation unprovenNEW
"Lauchlane McDonachie Campbell" among the unrelaxed rebels of 30 Nov 1613, listed immediately after the Tutor's filiated sons and immediately before his brother Duncan [RPC X:179]. Not explicitly filiated to the Tutor; possibly a further son, possibly household kin. His name and his brother's, read with the sibling chain extended one link beyond the text, are the likely source of the alternative trio (Dougall, Lachlan, Duncan) recalled in 2026 correspondence — note also the recurring Dugald/Lachlan cluster in the later laird generations (1722 entail: Dugald, Lachlan, Alexander). Untracked after 1613. [RPC X:179 · DC corr. · RBS3]
Campbell, Lillias (of Lawers), Lady McConochie · m. 1701 documented
Daughter of Sir James Campbell of Lawers; god-daughter of the Earl of Breadalbane; second wife (then widow) of Archibald of Inverawe. Her letters to Breadalbane survive in NRS GD112 (e.g. GD112/39/203/17). [DC ch.11 · NRS GD112]
Campbell, Patrick · the Tutor's brother, of DubhChoille documented
Tacksman of DubhChoille ("the dark wood," now Duchlie, east side of Loch Awe); supported the young laird against the Tutor, per tradition. Named among the unrelaxed rebels of 30 Nov 1613 as "Patrik McDonachie Campbell, 'father bruther' [paternal uncle] to the Laird of Innerraw," placed at Acharne with servitors of his own (Gillespik McCoull Dow VcKarne; John Dow McIntyre) [RPC X:179]. As "Patrick MacDonochie alias Campbell," styled uncle to Archibald of Inverawe, he renounced the two merk lands of Auchnacraig to Hugh MacCorquodale of Phantilands, 6 Nov 1617 [RD1/277, via RBS3] — a documented "alias" usage within the Tutor's immediate family. His son succeeded him at DubhChoille (sasine 1633). [RPC X:179 · RBS3 · DC · GM corr. · NRS-RD]
Campbell, Patrick · d. of Inverlochy wounds, 1645 documented
Younger son of the pupil; married Mary Cameron, the "Fair Maid of Callart"; father of John of Torgormaig. [DC]
Duncanson, Rev. John, minister at Kilmartin · c. 1630–1687 documented
Admitted 1655; married Beatrice Campbell of Stronchormaig 1656; deprived 1662 (Restoration ejections); restored under the Indulgence 1670; recruited for Argyll's 1685 Rising and fled to Holland, dying 1687. Wimberley queried "? a M'Conachie"; secondary accounts say Stirlingshire origin (unverified). Surname = direct anglicization of Mac Dhonnchaidh. [F · W · DC]
Duncanson, Major Robert · of the Argyll Regiment documented
Son of Rev. John Duncanson and Beatrice Campbell of Stronchormaig; transmitted the orders for the Glencoe Massacre, 1692. [F]
Graham, Barbara · the Tutor's widow documented
Second wife of the Tutor; mother of Iain og. Her bond of 15 July 1607 to George Schaw of Knockhill for 200 merks Scots, naming her son John [RD1/167], is the terminus for the Tutor's death and marks their departure from Argyll for the Lowlands. A further bond at Edinburgh, 1609. Her later moneylending may surface independently in the NRS Register of Deeds or Sheriff Court bonds. [DC · GM corr. · RBS3 · NRS-RD]
Maconochie, Allan, Lord Meadowbank · 1748–1816 tradition
Court of Session judge from 1796; his family acquired the Meadowbank estate, Midlothian, in the mid-1700s. The later Lord Meadowbank (Alexander) is the "chief of the clan" named in the 1867 McConihe memoir. Under the working hypothesis, cousins — not ancestors — of the American line. [IM · NRS NG3/4/31 · GM corr.]
McConihe, father of John · unknown, b. ~1630s–40s unknown — the research target
The open research question and the project's goal. Leading candidate pool, sharpened by direct reading of the 1613 entry: a son of one of the four outlawed McDonachie Campbell kinsmen — Archibald or Dougall (explicit sons of the Tutor), or Lachlan or Duncan (possible sons) — all unrelaxed rebels formally disclaimed by the Earl of Argyll on oath, 30 Nov 1613, and invisible to Scottish records thereafter. Outlawed men with no lord answering for them are structurally the likeliest emigrants; Ulster, where the Tutor had "friends" and (per CCSNA) had campaigned, is the natural destination. Secondary pool: untracked younger sons of the laird line (six sons of the pupil, d. bef. 1648). All documented main-line males of the window are accounted for in Scotland. Paths to him: Argyll parish registers; bundle 368 pedigrees; Ulster records under McConaghy-type forms; the original May–June 1613 hornings (Books of Adjournal); Y-DNA of a male-line McConihe descendant. [analysis of RPC X · DC · RBS3 · GM corr.]
McConihe, John, the immigrant · b. 22 Dec 1669, Argyllshire tradition → documented 1718
Family tradition: born Argyllshire 1669; household to Londonderry, Ireland, 1678; present in the 1689 siege; emigrated to Nutfield/Londonderry, N.H., 1718. Documented from arrival: home lot recorded 25 Feb 1722/3 as "McConoghy." [IM · BC · DB · ER II:80]
McConihe, Isaac · 1787–1867, Troy, N.Y. documented
Grandson of the immigrant (son of John of Merrimack); judge; his 1867 memorial volume preserves the family tradition — Inverawe, "Maconochy," the Argyll army command, and Meadowbank — in compressed and partly garbled form, each error traceable to a real event. [IM]

Part II — The name and its variants

FormContext
Mac DhonnchaidhGaelic, "son of Duncan." The kindred name of the Inverawe Campbells, from the 14th-century progenitor Duncan. Borne alongside the legal surname Campbell.
MacConnochie · M'Connoquhie · McConochie · McDonachie · McCondochieArgyll record spellings, 1500s–1600s. Often "alias Campbell" in charters ("Patrick MacDonochie alias Campbell," 1617, RD1/277; "John MacEwen VicEan VicDonachie galt alias Campbell of Clachbeache," 1640, RS10/1/f.510). In the 1613 Privy Council entry the laird is "McDonachie Campbell of Innerraw" and one man is "servitor to McCondochie" — the patronymic standing alone as the chief's name in a government record. "McConochie" alone also denotes the laird in the Earl of Argyll's correspondence. Caution: unrelated families carried similar live patronymics (e.g. Campbell of Oib; Loch Tay bonds).
DuncansonDirect English translation of Mac Dhonnchaidh. Borne by the Inveraray merchant family (conjectured MacConnochie stock, per DC) and by Rev. John Duncanson of Kilmartin.
MaconochieLowland scribal form; the Midlothian family of Meadowbank from the early 1700s.
McConaghy · MacConaughey · McConoghyUlster forms from the 1600s onward — "there the name is often spelt 'MacConaughey'" (DC p. 85). The form the immigrant carried to New England.
McConiheNew Hampshire scribal freezing of the Ulster pronunciation; first recorded "McConoghy" (1722/3), settling as McConihe in the Merrimack generation. The evolution runs entirely through 1720s Londonderry, N.H. town clerk entries — not an immigration officer.

Bibliographic note: The Red Book of Scotland (5th ed., 2025) carries no surname heading under any Mac Dhonnchaidh-derived form (site-wide and volume-content check, 12–13 July 2026); the Inverawe kindred is treated wholly within Campbell of Inverawe, Vol. 3. Genealogies filed by chiefly family, not by alias — a structural reason patronymic-bearing branches drop from view.
