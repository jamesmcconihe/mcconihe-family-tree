The MacConnochie–McConihe descent

One family, one name, four spellings, three countries. This chart lays out the working hypothesis for the ancestry of John McConihe (born 22 December 1669, Argyllshire; emigrated to Londonderry, New Hampshire, 1718), tracing the family tradition of descent from the MacConnochie Campbells of Inverawe. Every box is color-coded by the strength of its evidence, and every person links to the index below.

Status: links shown in amber or red are not established to genealogical proof standard. They mark where the evidence currently points and where research is active — not settled conclusions. Documented boxes rest on the independent, converging work of Diarmid Campbell and Gordon MacGregor plus primary records.

Index of persons

Duncan Campbell · progenitor, late 1300s
Eponym of the kindred — his descendants are Mac Dhonnchaidh, "sons of Duncan." Both modern scholars converge on Duncan, son of Gillespic (Archibald) Campbell of Lochawe; a Great Seal charter of 15 March 1369 names an ancestral Duncan in the barony of Lochawe. [DC · RBS3]
Archibald Campbell of Inverawe · fl. c. 1570
Laird of Inverawe; father of Dougall, John dubh, Patrick, and Duncan. [GM · DC]
Dougall Campbell of Inverawe · d. c. 1583
Laird; died leaving an infant heir, Archibald ("the pupil"), which brought his brother John dubh to power as Tutor. [DC]
John dubh MacConnochie Campbell, Tutor of Inverawe · c. 1557 – bef. July 1607
Guardian of Inverawe for ~20 years; ruled the kindred without owning it. In 1596 English intelligence reported him ready to lead 1,000 men to Ireland to aid Tyrone — he had "friends in Ireland." Said to have been killed by his nephew on the pupil's coming of age. [DC · GM]
Patrick & Duncan Campbell · the Tutor's brothers
Younger brothers of Dougall and John dubh. Patrick "in Auchreoch" (1597) possibly the Patrick in Duchollie whose son gave sasine in 1633. [DC · GM]
The lairds of Inverawe · 1607–1705, all traced
Archibald the pupil (d. bef. 1648, six younger sons); Lt-Col Dougall (second-in-command of Argyll's 1642 regiment for Ireland, d. 1666); Archibald, Governor of Duart, d.s.p. 21 May 1705. Estate passed via his brother Duncan of Crunachy's son; sold 1765. Every main-line male in the 1640s–80s is accounted for in Scotland. [DC · RBS3]
John Campbell, "Iain og" · son of the Tutor
Only legitimate son, by Barbara Graham. Named with his mother in her 1607 bond to George Schaw as she departed for the Lowlands. Married the widow of William M'Clellan of Mullock. No descendants traced — the root of the Meadowbank conjecture. [DC 84–85 · GM corr.]
Archibald Campbell · son of the Tutor, fl. 1613
Named with his brother Dugald, "sons to the tutor of Inverawe," in Colquhoun of Luss's Privy Council complaint of 30 Nov 1613 against Clan Gregor and their assisters. Untracked thereafter. Candidate ancestor of the Ulster line. [RPC · GM corr.]
Dugald Campbell · son of the Tutor, fl. 1613
As above — the second rebel brother; untracked after 1613. Candidate ancestor of the Ulster line. [RPC · GM corr.]
Maconochies of Meadowbank · Midlothian
Edinburgh legal family, documented from the early 1700s; acquired the Meadowbank estate mid-1700s. Their own tradition claims Argyll descent; no pre-1700 Argyll property record found (consistent with a landless origin). A pedigree sits in Argyll Papers bundle 368 at Inveraray. [GM corr. · ARG-11-02-109]
Allan Maconochie, Lord Meadowbank · 1748–1816
Court of Session judge from 1796; the title later held by his son Alexander — the "chief" named in the 1867 McConihe memoir. Under the hypothesis, cousins of the American line, not ancestors. [IM · NRS NG3/4/31]
John McConihe · b. 22 Dec 1669, Argyllshire
The immigrant ancestor. Birth date and Argyllshire origin from family tradition; his father is the open research question. [IM · BC · DB]
Londonderry, Ireland · 1678, tradition
Family tradition places the household's relocation to Derry in 1678 and John in the 1689 siege. Not in the 1867 memoir — carried by the Bureau County (1885) and DuBin streams. Ulster name form: McConaghy. [BC · DB]
Londonderry, New Hampshire · 1718, documented
John arrived with the Ulster-Scots settlers of Nutfield, 1718; home lot recorded 25 Feb 1722/3 as "M°Conoghy." [ER II:80]
New Hampshire descendants · to Isaac McConihe, 1787–1867
John of Merrimack, then Isaac McConihe of Troy, N.Y. (b. 22 Aug 1787), whose 1867 memorial volume preserves the family tradition — including the names Inverawe, Maconochy, and Meadowbank. [IM · ER]
